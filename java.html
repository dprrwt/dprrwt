Conditionals and Control Flow:

1. Introduction to Control Flow

This module of Java language is placed on Captain America and The First Avenger.


1. Learn Java

Scene: The discovery of Captain America's shield and the Tesseract in the ice.

Association: Just as this discovery unlocks a whole new realm of history and power, beginning with Java unlocks the potential of software development.
2. Introduction to Control Flow:

Scene: Steve Rogers' multiple attempts to enlist in the military.

Association: Just as there are different paths and outcomes based on Rogers' choices and situations, control flow determines the direction of a program based on conditions.
3. If-Then Statement:

Scene: Dr. Erskine's decision to recruit Rogers for the experimental program after seeing his spirit.

Association: "If" Steve has the right heart and spirit, "then" he gets selected. A simple condition leading to a consequent action.
4. If-Then-Else:

Scene: Steve's initial use as a mascot versus being sent to the front lines.

Association: "If" Steve can be used on the front lines, "then" he'll be a soldier; "else," he'll be a mascot. Two distinct outcomes based on one condition.
5. If-Then-Else-If:

Scene: Steve's transformation from mascot to hero.

Association: "If" Steve is seen as just a symbol, "then" he remains a mascot; "else if" he proves himself in battle, he becomes a hero. Multiple conditions leading to different outcomes.
6. Nested Conditional Statements:

Scene: Rogers' solo mission to rescue Bucky and other captured soldiers.

Association: Rogers made decisions based on multiple conditions. "If" he can get into the enemy base and "if" he finds Bucky, "then" he'll rescue him. One condition inside another.
7. Switch Statement:

Scene: Rogers forming his team to combat Hydra.

Association: Depending on each member's skills (e.g., sharpshooter, demolitions, etc.), Rogers assigns them roles, much like a switch statement checks different cases and executes corresponding actions.
